Scale is a very vague term. I have personally used it to define only engineering scale. But even here, there are multiple ways to define it.

At different stages of a company, it means different things. I was just reading a blog on Uber's MySQL migration. They mentioned that they currently have 2100 clusters with over 16,000 total nodes. That's a pretty insane scale.

But, for a small company, scale might mean something different. You don't even have to look at engineering scale. It could be the number of users you have.
For smaller companies, it might just be the number of users we are getting on a day-to-day basis. 

Scale is not something that one can look at in absolute terms. It is always relative to the current stage of the company. Scale can be in sales or marketing. How can I get more users? What's the most effective and efficient way to achieve this?

You might have to do things that don't scale. But it's for the betterment of the company.

I think, at the end of the day, it's about one thing.
**How do I create more value for the company at the lowest cost and highest efficiency?**

Isn't this the right question to ask ourselves?
