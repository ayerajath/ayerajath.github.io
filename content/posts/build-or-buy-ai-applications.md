To build or to buy? This is a question that I have encountered multiple times in my career.

Starting out my first job at Cardinal Health, we were undergoing this huge transition from a legacy system to a new system. It was an overhaul of the current analytics platform. This is where I first encountered the question of building or buying. As a new graduate, I always leaned towards building. Only later did I realize that this is not always the best option.

Building or buying depends on many factors. One of the most important factors is the **time to market**. If you're in a hurry to get something out, you might want to buy. If you have time, you might want to build. And this is just one factor. We have others like quality, cost, people, etc.

Before the AI revolution, we really had to question whether to build or buy traditional applications. The decision was harder because both options required significant investment.

But now we're in this interesting phase of AI. The question isn't just whether these AI companies' products are worth their price tags. It's about whether we even need their complete solutions.

The more I build AI systems, the more I realize that we're mostly just building wrappers around these LLMs These language models are getting so good that we can do amazing things with just their basic APIs. Take something like a customer service bot - instead of buying a complete solution, companies can build their own using GPT or Claude with just their specific requirements.

I see these big companies, ones that could easily afford to buy ready-made solutions, choosing to build instead. Why? Because building with AI is becoming dirt cheap and works great for their specific use cases. Each application can be customized exactly how they want it. How can one vendor's solution answer all these different use cases? That's why companies are building their own.

It's just an integration with your favorite LLM. And you can do so much with it. You get the results, you can customize them, you can add your own logic to it, play with it. Just another integration! We shall be better integrators for sure. Something to take from this. 

Hats off to companies like OpenAI, Anthropic, Google, etc. They're making it incredibly easy for us to build these applications. They've given us the LEGO blocks - now we just need to put them together in ways that make sense for our needs.

The build vs. buy question isn't going away, but with AI, the scales are definitely tipping towards 'build' for me.
